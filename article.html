How learning about my legal rights changed everything

Rights

June 10, 2025

I grew up in Sindhupalchok, where nobody talked about women's rights. When I was 16, my family arranged my marriage. I didn't know I had the right to say no, that Nepal's law sets the minimum age at 20. When a volunteer from Elevate visited our village and told me about the legal protections I had, it changed my entire life. I refused the marriage. My family was angry for months. But today I am in grade 12 and planning to study law...

Breaking the silence around Chhaupadi in our community

Awareness

June 7, 2025

In our village in Achham, Chhaupadi was never questioned — it was simply what we did. Girls would stay in cold sheds during menstruation. Two girls in nearby villages died from snake bites in those sheds. After attending an Elevate awareness session, five of us young women decided to start talking openly about this. We spoke to the elders, shared the medical facts. It took six months but our village officially ended the practice last month...

S
Sita T. — Achham

5 myths about gender equality in Nepal — debunked

Awareness

May 28, 2025

Myth 1: "This is just a Western concept." False — Nepal's own constitution (Article 18 and 38) guarantees gender equality and special protections for women. Myth 2: "Men can't be victims of gender discrimination." False — rigid gender norms harm men and boys too. Myth 3: "It's improving on its own." False — the gender gap in education and income has actually widened in rural areas since 2020...
